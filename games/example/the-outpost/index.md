You're out. Pine air hits your lungs like cold water. Behind you the facility squats against the hillside, silent now. Smoke or mist — you can't tell — curls from a vent on the roof.

The service road leads north through the trees. Half a kilometre up, a concrete building with an antenna on its roof. If anything out here still works, that's your best shot.

  A figure catches up behind you — one of the freed prisoners, out of breath but steady on their feet. "I followed the road," they say. "Hoped someone else made it this far." They fall into step beside you.
WITNESS_ESCAPE?
  You're alone. The road behind you is empty. You think about the faces at the cell windows. The one who mouthed *please*. You had no choice. You keep telling yourself that.
