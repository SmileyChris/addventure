You wake up bound to a chair. Your wrists burn against coarse rope. The air tastes of rust and stale electricity. A row of dead monitors lines the far wall, their screens dark and cracked. Somewhere above, a fluorescent tube flickers and hums.

Nobody is coming for you. The door has no handle on this side. Whatever happened here, it happened fast — papers are scattered across the floor, a chair is overturned, and something dark is smeared across the wall by the exit. You don't want to think about what it is.

You need to get out. Use what you can find. Trust nothing.
